Pentathlon: Games for the Skilled

History

Pentathlon is a sport contest consisting of 5 events, and one of the oldest games part of the Ancient Olympics. The first ever documented Pentathlon was in 708 BC in Ancient Greece. In mythology, it is said that Jason is the creator of the Pentathlon and naming his frined Pelus as the first ever winner. Pentathlon has made it over countless centuries and is still prelevant till this day in the Olympics, even having variations of it. Due to the numerous amount of skills needed, pentathletes were seen and held to a high standard as prime physical specimens. By the 77th Olympics, the events were usually done in a trio of events, followed up by 2 individual events explained later on.

Rules

In the Ancient Pentathlon, the format varied depending on the shedule and events. These 5 events were contested in the same day and generally consist of: long jump, javelin throwing, discus throwing, stadion (short foot race) and wrestling.

Long Jump

Competitors run down a runway/path as their initial approach and attempt to jummp and leap as far away from where they first jumped. Each competitor has 3 tries.

Javelin Throwing

Within a preset area, competitors do an inital short run, gaining momentum and throw a spear of about 8 ft 2 in in length as far as they possibly can.

Discus Throwing

Also known as disc throw, competitors throw a heavy disk weighing on average 2 kg, attempting to mark a farther distance than others.

Stadion Race

This is a short sprinting race in wich competitors run a 192 m long stadium barefooted. Out of all the numerous running events, the stadion was seen as the most prestigous.

Wrestling

Wrestling is the oldest form of fighting without weapons. To win a match, 3 points needed to be scored. For a player to score a point, his opponent had to either touch the ground with his back/hip/shoulder, concede defeat by submission-hold or forced out of ring.
