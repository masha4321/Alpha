Pentathlon: Games for the Skilled

History

Pentathlon is a sport contest consisting of 5 events, and one of the oldest games part of the Ancieny Olympics. The first ever documented Pentathlon was in 708 BC in Ancient Greece. In mythology, it is said that Jason is the creator of the Pentathlon and naming his frined Pelus as the first ever winner. Pentathlon has made it over countless centuries and is still prelevant till this day in the Olympics, even having variations of it. Due to the numerous amount of skills needed, pentathletes were seen and held to a high standard as prime physical specimens. By the 77th Olympics, the events were usually done in a trio (long jump, javelin throw, discuss throw), followed up by stadion foot race, and wrestlin as the finale.
