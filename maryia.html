Dolichos: Long Distance Running (Δόλιχος)

History

The dolichos was an ancient Greek race introduced in 720BC and held at three of the four Panhellenic festivals, the ancient Olympic Games, Pythian Games and Nemean Games. It's a 4800m long race and the event is similar to the modern "marathon". It was the longest race among Greek athletic festivals.

According to the philosopher Philostratus, the dolichos was not as intense as we can imagine: the speed was only a little faster than walking speed. It served more as background entertainment for the spectators.

The prestige of winning the race was much more significant than the prize he received, which was an olive branch.

The dolichos is believed to have originated from the distances covered by the messengers that delivered news and messages throughout Greece.

The sport was abolished in 394AD.

Rules

The race took place on a track called stadiion, located in the stadium. The athletes would line up at the beggining of the track, nude,barefoot and waiting for the judge's take off signal. The first runner to complete the laps won the title.

Interesting facts

You can participate in a modern Dolichos in Texas. Visit their website!

After the conquest of Greece by the Roman Emperor Theodosius I around 393-394 AD, the Greek Olympics were officially banned by the emperor.

Acanthus of Sparta was the first recorded winner of the Dolichos, and according to some sources, he was the first one who ran naked in these games.
