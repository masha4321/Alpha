Ancient Olympic Game--TETHRIPPON



What is Tethrippon

Tethrippon, a four-horse chariot, one kind of the chariot races, was said to be the event that founded the Olympic Games in Greece. It was first introduced to Olympic back in 680 BC.It was treated more important than horse back riding race back that time; There are other kinds of chariot racing, with 2 hourses or 6 hourses, but tethrippon is the most popular one. Chariot racing was very dangerous to both drivers and horses. They often sufferred serious injury and even death. It was said the most dangerous Game in Olympic.More



Rule

Usually four to six chariots competed in a single race, normally consisting of seven laps around the circus. It's held in a specially built arena, or hippodrome, with posts amrking the turning points. As many as 10 chariots raced at a time. In some form of the chariot race, the warriors had to leap from a moving chariot, run beside it and then leap back in.



Fun Facts &Influece


Winner's Prize: The winner of the 4 hourses Chariot race(Tethrippon) was awarded 140 ceramic pots full of olive oil, a particular extravagant Prize.

Influence: It was said that the modern Automoble or other vehcile racing are orignated from tethrippon or chariot race.
